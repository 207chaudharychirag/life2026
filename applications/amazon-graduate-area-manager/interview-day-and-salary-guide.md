# First Interview Guide + Salary Conversation — Amazon Graduate Area Manager

This is your first official interview — the goal is simple: stay calm, be yourself,
and tell clear stories. You already have the experience; this is just about showing it.

---

## Part 1 — Before the interview

**If it's virtual (most Amazon first rounds are):**
- Test your camera, mic and internet 15 min early. Use headphones.
- Quiet, tidy, well-lit room. Face a window or light, not with it behind you.
- Have water, your resume, the job description, and your STAR notes nearby (off-camera).
- Log in 5 minutes early.

**If in person:** arrive 10–15 min early, smart-casual/business dress, bring a printed resume.

**Mindset:** It's a conversation, not an interrogation. Nerves are normal — slow down,
breathe, and it's fine to pause and think before answering.

---

## Part 2 — How the interview will flow

1. **Small talk / intro** (2–3 min) — be warm, smile.
2. **"Tell me about yourself"** — your 60–90 second pitch (script below).
3. **Behavioural questions** (the core) — STAR answers tied to Leadership Principles.
   See `interview-prep-STAR.md` for your ready answers.
4. **Your questions for them** — always have 2–3 ready (below).
5. **Close** — thank them, reaffirm your interest.

### Your "Tell me about yourself" pitch (practise out loud)
> "I'm completing my MBA at RMIT, and alongside it I've been working in a Woolworths
> Customer Fulfilment Centre for nearly two years. So I've got hands-on fulfilment and
> logistics experience — picking multi-order runs across ambient, chilled and frozen,
> hitting tight run times, training new starters and jumping in to help the team finish.
> My MBA has built the analytical and strategic side — including a supply-chain consulting
> project with Cargill. I'm drawn to the Graduate Area Manager role because it combines
> exactly that: leading a team on the floor and using data to improve operations. I'm
> also genuinely happy with night shift."

---

## Part 3 — The salary conversation

**Key fact:** Graduate program salaries are usually **fixed and standardised** across the
cohort, so there is normally little room to negotiate base pay. Play this calmly.

**If they ask "What are your salary expectations?"**
> "I understand graduate program salaries are usually set, so I'm comfortable with the
> standard package for this role. I'm more focused on the opportunity, the training, and
> growing into the operation. Could you share the range and how the night-shift allowance
> works?"

This shows you're informed, reasonable, and not difficult — while still getting them to
reveal the numbers.

**If they make an offer:**
- Don't accept on the spot. Say: *"Thank you, I'm really excited. Could I take a day or
  two to review the full details?"*
- Then ask about the **total package**, not just base: night-shift allowance, any sign-on
  bonus, RSUs/stock, super (superannuation), shift pattern, and **start date**.

**What you CAN reasonably discuss (vs. base):** start date (to align with your ~Aug 2026
work rights / 485 visa), sign-on, and location.

**Pay reference (Australia, approximate):**
- Indeed average for Amazon Area Manager (AU): ~AUD $95,000/yr (experienced).
- Graduate base estimate: ~AUD $75,000–$90,000, plus night-shift allowance and possible
  RSUs/sign-on → realistic total ~$90,000–$110,000+.
- These are estimates — let the recruiter confirm the actual band. Don't quote a hard
  number first; ask them for the range.

## Part 4 — Questions to ask THEM (pick 2–3)
- What does the 11-week onboarding look like day to day?
- What does success look like for a Graduate Area Manager in the first 6 months?
- How big is the team I'd be leading, and what's the career path after the program?
- **Is there a Victoria/Melbourne placement for this cohort, or is it NSW-based?** (ASK EARLY)
- What's the program start date?

---

## Part 5 — IMPORTANT: location

You've said you don't want to leave Melbourne, but the role you applied to is in
**Kemps Creek, NSW (Sydney)**. Raise this with the recruiter on the first call:
> "I'm Melbourne-based — is there a Victoria placement option for this program, or is this
> cohort specifically NSW? I want to make sure we're aligned on location."

If it's NSW-only and you won't relocate, apply instead to a **VIC-based Graduate Area
Manager** req on amazon.jobs (filter by Victoria). Better to clarify now than late.

---

## Part 6 — Common questions to rehearse
- Why Amazon? Why operations?
- Why are you OK with night shift? (Focus, fewer distractions, used to warehouse hours.)
- A time you handled conflict / a difficult person.
- A time you failed and what you learned.
- A time you had to learn something quickly.
- How do you prioritise when everything is urgent?

## Quick do's and don'ts
- DO speak in "I", give specific examples, end answers with a result.
- DO be honest about your visa timeline (485 from ~Aug 2026) if asked.
- DON'T memorise word-for-word — know the story, speak naturally.
- DON'T badmouth past employers.
- DON'T hard-negotiate a fixed graduate salary — stay collaborative.
