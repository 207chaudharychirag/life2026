# Online Assessment Prep — Amazon Graduate Area Manager

You've passed the resume screen. The online assessment (~50–60 min) is the next gate.
Deadline: ~2 weeks. Do it sooner rather than later (Amazon hires on a rolling basis).

---

## Logistics
- Set aside 50–60 min, quiet room, reliable internet, laptop/desktop (not phone).
- Don't click "Start" until you're ready — it's timed once you begin.
- You can take short breaks between modules; don't repeatedly refresh.
- Need more time? You can email the recruiter for an extension (may slow your candidacy).
- You'll get an email confirmation when it's submitted successfully.

---

## What the assessment usually contains
1. **Work Style Assessment** — "which is more like you" statements; measures Leadership-Principle fit.
2. **Work Sample / Virtual Job Simulation** — realistic "day as an Area Manager" scenarios.
3. **Data / multi-source interpretation** — read charts/metrics, choose the data-supported action.
4. **Manage-your-day / prioritisation** — rank competing tasks by urgency + impact.

---

## The decision rules that win scenario questions (in priority order)
1. SAFETY FIRST — always. Never trade safety/wellbeing for speed or output.
2. CUSTOMER IMPACT — protect the customer experience next.
3. USE THE DATA — gather facts / dive deep before deciding; don't assume.
4. TAKE OWNERSHIP — handle it yourself where you can; escalate only genuinely serious issues.
5. DEVELOP THE TEAM — coach and support associates rather than blame.
6. BIAS FOR ACTION — act promptly on the right thing.

Prioritisation order: safety/risk -> customer-blocking issues -> time-sensitive deadlines -> rest.

---

## Work Style Assessment tips
- Answer **consistently** — similar questions appear more than once to catch contradictions.
- Lean toward: taking initiative, owning outcomes, high standards, helping teammates,
  staying calm under pressure, being comfortable with fast change and data.
- Be authentic — don't max out "strongly agree" on everything; a few balanced answers
  read as more honest.
- There are no trick questions, but there IS a profile they're looking for (an operations
  leader who is action-oriented, people-focused and standards-driven).

## Scenario module tips
- Read the full scenario before answering.
- If unsure between two "good" options, pick the one that is safer / more customer-focused
  / more data-driven.
- Favour responses where YOU act and follow up, over passive or blame options.
- Balance speed with quality — Amazon wants results AND high standards, not shortcuts.

## Data module tips
- Identify what the question actually asks before reading the chart.
- Look for the metric that's off-target / the trend that matters.
- Choose the action the numbers support — don't over-interpret.
- Watch units and time periods.

---

## Mindset
- This stage is about judgement, not knowledge — there's nothing to "study," just understand
  what good operational decisions look like (the rules above).
- Your real CFC experience (safety, accuracy, helping the team, hitting targets) is exactly
  the judgement they're testing for. Trust it.
