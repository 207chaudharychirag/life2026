# Interview Prep — Amazon Graduate Area Manager (STAR)

Amazon interviews are **behavioural** and scored against the **Leadership Principles (LPs)**.
Answer every "Tell me about a time…" question with **STAR**:
- **S**ituation — set the scene briefly
- **T**ask — what was your responsibility / the goal
- **A**ction — what *you* (not "we") did, step by step
- **R**esult — the outcome, ideally with a number or clear improvement

Tips: speak in "I", keep S+T short (~20%), spend most time on A, always finish with R.
Have a "what I learned" line ready — Amazon loves reflection (Learn and Be Curious).

## Ready-to-use STAR answers

### 1. Customer Obsession — "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer."
- **S:** At the Woolworths CFC, an item on a customer's order was out of stock / not on the shelf.
- **T:** My job was to keep the order as complete and accurate as possible so the customer wasn't let down.
- **A:** Rather than just marking it missing, I checked for a suitable substitute and located short-picked items, and flagged the gap so the right call could be made on the order.
- **R:** The order went out as complete as possible and on time, protecting the customer's experience. It reinforced for me that small decisions on the floor directly affect the customer at the door.

### 2. Ownership — "Tell me about a time you took responsibility beyond your role."
- **S:** On shifts where I'd completed my own picking runs but still had time left.
- **T:** Nobody told me to — but the wider team still had open runs to finish before cut-off.
- **A:** I took on "rush picks," jumping onto colleagues' outstanding runs to help close them out faster, across ambient, chilled and frozen zones.
- **R:** The team consistently hit its daily completion targets, and I became someone the team relied on when things got tight.

### 3. Deliver Results — "Tell me about a time you hit a tough target under pressure."
- **S:** CFC runs are time-sensitive — multi-order trolleys (up to 9 orders, ~85 items) against set run times.
- **T:** I needed to pick accurately *and* beat the clock, consistently.
- **A:** I worked methodically using item images and article numbers to find products fast and avoid backtracking, and stayed focused through long runs.
- **R:** I regularly completed runs ahead of target time while keeping accuracy high — proving I can hold both speed and quality at once.

### 4. Insist on High Standards / Dive Deep — "Tell me about a time you improved quality or caught an error."
- **S:** Mispicks are the main quality risk in fulfilment.
- **T:** I wanted to keep my accuracy high in a fast, high-volume environment.
- **A:** I made a habit of verifying both the item image and the article number before picking, rather than relying on one cue.
- **R:** This kept my mispicks low and orders accurate. It taught me that a simple, consistent check beats rushing and reworking.

### 5. Develop Others / Earn Trust — "Tell me about a time you helped someone improve."
- **S:** New starters joined the CFC and, like everyone at first, took time to get up to speed.
- **T:** I helped get them confident and productive.
- **A:** I showed them the picking process, the zone layout, and the accuracy habits that worked for me, and supported them while they found their pace.
- **R:** They're now solid, contributing team members. I learned I genuinely enjoy developing people — which is the core of the Area Manager role.

### 6. Bias for Action — "Tell me about a time you acted quickly without being told."
- **S:** During busy periods with dispatch handovers needed and teams stretched.
- **T:** Keep the operation moving without waiting to be asked.
- **A:** I stepped into Direct-to-Boot (P2P) dispatch handovers when needed and supported the night dispatch and inventory teams.
- **R:** Handovers and dispatch stayed on schedule; I built a reputation for stepping up across functions, not just my own task.

### 7. Think Big / Are Right, A Lot — "Tell me about a time you used data/analysis to recommend a change."
- **S:** In my MBA, a consulting project with **Cargill** on AI-enabled supply chain.
- **T:** Recommend improvements to their supply-chain and logistics operations.
- **A:** I ran stakeholder analysis and a governance assessment, then built data-backed recommendations. (Also: a Deloitte data-analytics simulation using modelling and Tableau.)
- **R:** Delivered clear, evidence-based recommendations — showing I can zoom out from the floor to the bigger operational picture, which is what Area Managers do when driving process improvement.
