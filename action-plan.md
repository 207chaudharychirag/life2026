## Month 2 — July 2026: Job Hunt at Full Speed

**Theme: Maximum output on applications + interview prep**

### Milestones
- [ ] 30+ total applications sent
- [ ] At least 3 interviews booked
- [ ] 1 portfolio project or work sample ready
- [ ] Sleep and exercise routine consistent (5/7 days)

### Weekly Breakdown

**Week 1–2**
- 10 applications/week (quality over quantity — tailor each cover letter)
- 1 hr/day interview prep: behavioural questions, role-specific knowledge
- Research target companies deeply before applying

**Week 3–4**
- Mock interviews (use a friend, record yourself, or use AI tools)
- Follow up on all unanswered applications (polite email/LinkedIn message)
- Build or refresh 1 portfolio piece relevant to your target role
- Evaluate part-time job: is it helping your resume or just paying bills?

---

## Month 3 — August 2026: Momentum & Skill Building

**Theme: Keep applying, start building real skills**

### Milestones
- [ ] 50+ total applications sent
- [ ] At least 1 offer or advanced interview stage
- [ ] 1 online course started (relevant to job target)
- [ ] Financial budget created and tracked

### Weekly Breakdown

**Week 1–2**
- Continue applying (10/week minimum)
- Start a short course (Coursera, Udemy, YouTube — pick ONE, finish it)
- Reflect: what roles are getting responses vs. silence? Adjust strategy

**Week 3–4**
- If interviews coming in: prep hard, research companies, practice answers
- If no responses: rewrite resume, get feedback, try different job boards
- Set up a simple monthly budget — track income (part-time) vs. expenses
- One social activity/week — don't isolate during the job hunt grind

---

## Month 4 — September 2026: Transition & Settle

**Theme: Land the job OR double down with a plan B**

### Milestones
- [ ] Job offer accepted (target) OR clear plan B defined
- [ ] Wrap up part-time role gracefully if new job secured
- [ ] Fitness habit — 3x/week consistent
- [ ] Read 1 book (career, mindset, or finance)

### Weekly Breakdown

**Week 1–2**
- If job offer: negotiate salary, confirm start date, prepare for onboarding
- If still searching: expand search (different cities, remote roles, adjacent roles)
- Reassess: should you upskill more or apply to more places?

**Week 3–4**
- Transition period — wrap up part-time cleanly, get a reference
- Start new job OR enrol in a more structured upskilling program
- Review your 4-month progress: what worked, what didn't

---

## Month 5 — October 2026: Build & Grow

**Theme: Stabilise new job, invest in yourself**

### Milestones
- [ ] Settled into new role — first 30 days strong
- [ ] Side skill/project continued (don't stop learning)
- [ ] Emergency savings started (even small)
- [ ] Clear social/personal boundaries at work

### Weekly Breakdown

**Week 1–2**
- New job: listen more than you speak, understand team dynamics
- Take notes on everything — processes, people, tools
- Keep exercise routine — work stress is real

**Week 3–4**
- Start contributing ideas at work (carefully, respectfully)
- Set a monthly savings goal, even if small
- Reconnect with friends/family — celebrate the win

---

## Month 6 — November 2026: Review & Level Up

**Theme: Consolidate, reflect, plan the next 6 months**

### Milestones
- [ ] 3-month work review — know where you stand
- [ ] Identify 1 promotion/growth path at new job
- [ ] Personal skills audit — what's next to learn?
- [ ] Written reflection on the last 6 months
- [ ] New 6-month plan drafted

### Weekly Breakdown

**Week 1–2**
- Self-assessment: am I growing at this job?
- Identify a mentor or senior colleague to learn from
- Review finances: income vs. expenses, savings rate

**Week 3–4**
- Write your 6-month reflection (what you did, what changed, what you'd do differently)
- Set goals for December 2026 – May 2027
- Celebrate. Seriously. This was a lot of work.

## Weekly Check-In (Every Sunday)

Ask yourself:
1. Did I complete my top priorities this week?
2. What did I avoid that I shouldn't have?
3. What's the ONE most important thing for next week?
4. How is my energy/mood? Do I need to adjust?
